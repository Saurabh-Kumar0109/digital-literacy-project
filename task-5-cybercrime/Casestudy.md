### **Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud**

###### 



#### ***Type of Cybercrime***<i>: **UPI / Online Payment Fraud**</i>





**Prepared by: Saurabh Kumar(25BSA10120)**

**CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | VIT Bhopal University**





###### **The Incident**



In October 2024, Priya, a second-year B.Tech student from Pune, listed her old

engineering textbooks for sale on OLX. Within hours, she received a WhatsApp

message from a buyer named "Rahul" who agreed to pay ₹1,200 immediately via

Google Pay.



Rahul sent a screenshot showing he had "initiated" the payment and asked Priya

to accept it by entering her UPI PIN. Trusting the screenshot, Priya opened her

Google Pay app and entered her PIN. Within seconds, ₹8,500 — her entire month's

pocket money — was debited from her account.





###### **How It Happened — Step by Step**



**Step 1 — Contact:**

The attacker found Priya's listing on OLX and contacted

her on WhatsApp, posing as a genuine buyer.



**Step 2 — Fake Screenshot:**

He sent a fabricated payment confirmation

screenshot to create a false sense of trust and urgency.



**Step 3 — The Trap:**

He told Priya to "accept" the payment by entering her

UPI PIN — exploiting her lack of awareness that a PIN is only required to

send money, never to receive it.



**Step 4 — Deduction:**

The moment Priya entered her PIN, a pre-prepared

collect request was fulfilled, draining her account.



**Step 5 — Disappearance:**

Rahul immediately blocked Priya on WhatsApp and

the number became unreachable.



###### 

###### **Who Is Targeted**



College students selling items on OLX, Quikr, or Facebook Marketplace are

prime targets because they are eager to complete transactions quickly and are

often unaware of how UPI collect requests work. First-time sellers between

18–22 years old are the most vulnerable group.





###### **Consequences**



Priya lost ₹8,500 — money she could not recover because the transaction was

completed using her own PIN and was therefore classified as "authorized" by

the bank. Beyond the financial loss, she experienced stress, embarrassment,

and a lasting distrust of digital payment platforms.





###### **Key Lesson**



**Your UPI PIN is only required to SEND money**. You never need to enter it

to receive a payment. Any request to **"enter your PIN to accept money"** is

fraud — disconnect immediately and report to the National Cyber Crime Helpline

at 1930 or file a complaint at **cybercrime.gov.in.**
