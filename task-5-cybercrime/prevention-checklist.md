#### **Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist for College Students**





###### **General Online Safety-**



&#x20;**\[1]. Use strong, unique passwords\*\* for every account.** Use a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.

&#x20;     Never reuse your college email password for social media or shopping sites.



&#x20;**\[2].** **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on Gmail, Instagram, and all financial apps. This adds a second layer of security even if your password is stolen.



&#x20;**\[3]. Never click on links from unknown senders** in SMS, WhatsApp, or email. These links may lead to fake login pages designed to steal your credentials(phishing sites).



&#x20;**\[4]. Download apps only from official stores** (Google Play Store / Apple App Store). Avoid APK files shared via WhatsApp or Telegram — they may contain malware.



&#x20;**\[5]. Log out of shared devices.** If you use a college computer lab, library system, or a friend's phone, always sign out of your accounts before leaving.

&#x20;

&#x20;**\[6]. Keep your device software updated.** System updates often include security patches that fix known vulnerabilities exploited by attackers.





###### **Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)**

&#x20;

&#x20;**\[7]. Your UPI PIN is ONLY used to SEND money — never to RECEIVE it.** If anyone tells you to enter your PIN to "accept" a payment, it is fraud. Hang up or disconnect immediately.



&#x20;**\[8]. Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or CVV with anyone**, including people claiming to be bank representatives, scholarship portals, or college seniors.

&#x20;     Legitimate institutions will never ask for these details.



###### 

###### &#x20;**Social Media \& Identity Safety**



&#x20;**\[9]. Do not share your Aadhaar number, college ID number, or date of birth** on any public platform. This information is sufficient for identity theft.



&#x20;**\[10]. Verify before you trust.** If you receive an urgent message claiming to be from a college authority or scholarship body, verify it by calling the official number listed on your                             college's website.
