### **Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students**



##### **DO's**



&#x20;**1. Keep your profiles professional.**

&#x20;	Avoid posting your home address, phone number, college ID, or financial details on any public platform.

&#x20;**2. Think before you post.**

&#x20;  	Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or a future employer saw this?" If the answer is no, do not post it.

&#x20;**3. Use privacy settings wisely.**

&#x20;	Regularly review who can see your posts, photos, and personal information. Keep personal accounts private if you wish to share casual content.

&#x20;**4. Give credit when sharing others' work.**

&#x20;	Tag original creators and include sources when sharing articles, images, or ideas that are not your own.

&#x20;**5. Engage positively and constructively.**

&#x20;	Join academic groups, follow industry pages, and participate in meaningful discussions that help build your knowledge and network.

&#x20;**6. Report harassment or harmful content.**

&#x20; 	Use the platform's built-in reporting tools if you encounter bullying, hate speech, or misinformation. Do not engage with or amplify harmful posts.



##### **DON'Ts**



&#x20;**1. Do not share personal information publicly.**

&#x20; 	Avoid posting your home address, phone number, college ID, or financial details on any public platform.

&#x20;**2. Do not post or forward unverified news.**

&#x09;Spreading misinformation - even unintentionally - can have serious consequences. Always check a source before sharing

&#x20;**3. Do not use social media to bully, mock, or demean others.**

&#x20;	Cyberbullying is a punishable offence under Indian IT law. Treat others online exactly as you would in person.

&#x20;**4. Do not accept connection or friend requests from strangers.**

&#x09;Unknown individuals requesting access to your profile may have malicious intent, including phishing or identity theft

&#x20;**5. Do not post content while emotional or impulsive.**

&#x09;Posts made in anger or frustration are often regretted. Draft, pause, and review before publishing anything sensitive.



##### **Quick Reference Table**



|**Situation**|**Recommended Action**|
